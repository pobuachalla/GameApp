1. Getting Started

Open index.html in any modern mobile or desktop browser. The app works entirely offline — no internet connection is required after the initial load. Your match data is automatically saved to the browser's local storage, so you can close the browser and return without losing progress.

When you first open the app you see the pre-match screen: a timer at 00:00, both scores at 0—0, and a pitch grid with numbered player positions (greyed out until the match starts).

Pre-match screen — tap the green play button to begin

2. The Main Screen

The main screen is divided into four areas, from top to bottom:

Timer Card — Displays the current period (1st Half / 2nd Half), match clock, a play/pause button, and an End Half or End Match button.

Scoreboard — Shows your team's score on the left and the opposition on the right, each in Goals—Points format with a total in brackets. Tap the pencil icon beside either score to manually adjust it.

Player Grid — A pitch-layout grid showing all starting positions (1–15 or 1–13). Each button shows the player's initials and their running score tally. Tap a player to record an action.

Bottom Bar — Four buttons: Setup, Log, Stats, and Undo.

Mid-match — 2nd Half, Donaghmore/Ashbourne 2-9 vs Dunboyne 1-12. Note player initials, score tallies, yellow card on #6, sub dot on #7.

3. Setup — Teams, Players & Settings

Tap Setup in the bottom bar to open the Setup panel. This is where you configure everything before (or during) a match.

Setup panel — team names, sport toggle, team size, and starting players

Team Names

Enter your team name in the Us field and the opposition in the Opposition field. These names appear on the scoreboard and in all reports.

Location & Referee

Optional fields for the match venue and referee name. These are included in printed/shared reports.

Sport Toggle

Tap the sport toggle to switch between Hurling (sliotar icon, left) and Football (ball icon, right). This affects whether the "2 Point" scoring option is available (Football only), whether "From 65" or "From 45" appears, whether "Chop" appears as a free reason (Hurling only), and restart labels ("Puck Out" vs "Kick Out").

Team Size Toggle

Switch between 15-a-side and 13-a-side formats. This changes the pitch grid layout and the number of starting player slots.

Shot Location Tracking

At the bottom of the Setup panel, a Shot Location Tracking toggle (indicated by a small on/off icon) enables zone tagging for shots. When active, every Goal, Point, 2-Point, and Wide triggers an extra step where you tap a cell on a full-pitch zone grid to record where the shot was taken from. The toggle is off by default — enable it only when you have a dedicated person tracking. Collected data feeds the Shot Map in the Stats panel and adds zone columns to the CSV export.

Starting Players

Below the toggles is a numbered list of player slots. Slot 1 is always the goalkeeper (marked with a gold badge). Type each player's name into the corresponding field. The app automatically generates initials for the pitch grid — it intelligently handles Irish names with O' prefixes and disambiguates clashing initials.

The © icon beside each player lets you designate a captain. Tap it to toggle — the active captain shows a green © and a badge appears on their pitch button.

Bench

Bench section — slot 16 is the sub GK, 17+ are outfield subs

Scroll down past the starting players to find the Bench section. Slot 16 is the substitute goalkeeper. Slots 17+ are outfield subs. The bench list automatically expands as you add names — type a name in the last empty slot and a new one appears.

4. Running a Match

Starting the Match

With your setup complete, return to the main screen and tap the green Play ▶ button. This starts the 1st Half timer and enables the player grid. A "1st Half started" event is logged automatically.

The clock counts upward from 00:00. After 30 minutes, the display begins flashing red to indicate added/injury time.

Ending the First Half

Tap End Half (amber button on the right) during the 1st Half. A confirmation dialog appears. On confirming, the timer stops, a half-time event is logged, and a Half-Time Report modal appears showing the score, shooting efficiency, and top scorers. Tap Continue to dismiss it.

Starting the Second Half

Tap the green ▶▶ button to begin the 2nd Half. The clock resets to 00:00 and starts counting again.

Ending the Match

Tap End Match (red button) during the 2nd Half and confirm. The app shows a Full-Time modal with the final score, result (Win/Loss/Draw), and a complete scorers summary. After dismissing, the Undo button transforms into a Reset button.

5. Recording Player Actions

While the timer is running, tap any player button on the pitch grid. An action selection modal appears:

Action modal for C. Walsh (#11) — grouped into Scores, Discipline, and Personnel

Actions are grouped into three categories:

Scores

  • Goal — Records a goal (+1 to the goals column). You'll be asked how it was scored: From Play, From Free, From Penalty, or From 65/45.
  • Point — Records a point (+1 to points). Same follow-up options.
  • 2 Point — Football only. A 2-pointer scored over the crossbar from outside the 45m arc.
  • Wide — Records a wide. Same follow-up for how it was attempted.

After any score or wide, if Shot Location Tracking is enabled, a full-screen zone picker appears first. The pitch is divided into a 5-column × 7-row grid. Tap the cell where the shot was taken — the attacking end is always at the bottom. Cells along the 45m/65m line and the penalty spot are pre-selected automatically when relevant. Tap Select to confirm or Skip to record without a location. The Restart dialog follows.

Discipline

  • Free — Records a free conceded. Follow-up asks the reason: Push, Chop, Steps, Tackle, Pickup, Throw, or Other.
  • Advanced — Records an advanced mark or advantage played.
  • Yellow Card — Adds a yellow card indicator to the player's button.
  • Black Card — Adds a black card indicator (Football).
  • Red Card — Adds a red card and disables the player's button for the rest of the match.

Possession

  • Turnover Won — Records a possession gained (green, up-arrow icon).
  • Turnover Lost — Records a possession lost (red, down-arrow icon).

Turnovers feed into the Momentum calculation in Stats, contributing positively or negatively depending on direction.

Personnel

  • Substitution — Initiates the substitution flow (see Section 7).

After recording a score, the player's button updates to show their running tally (e.g. 0-5 for five points).

6. Adjusting Scores

Tap the pencil icon beside either team's score on the scoreboard to open the score adjustment modal.

Score adjustment for the opposition — add/remove goals, points, or record a wide

From here you can add or remove goals, points, and 2-pointers (Football), or record a wide. When adding a score, you'll be asked how it was scored (From Play, From Free, etc.), followed by the restart prompt.

Tip: This is the primary way to record opposition scores, since opposition players don't have buttons on the pitch grid. It's also useful for correcting your own team's score.

7. Substitutions

To make a substitution:

  1. Tap the player you want to bring off on the pitch grid.
  2. Select Substitution from the action modal.
  3. A "Who comes on?" modal lists all available bench players. Goalkeepers can only be replaced by the sub goalkeeper (slot 16), and vice versa.
  4. Tap the incoming player.

The substitution is logged, and the pitch button updates to show the new player's initials with a green dot indicating they are a sub. The substituted-off player becomes available for re-entry later (listed as "Previously subbed off").

8. The Event Log

Tap Log in the bottom bar to open the event log.

Event log — 28 events with timestamps, badges, and descriptions. Export CSV and WhatsApp buttons at the bottom.

Every action is recorded chronologically with a timestamp, badge (player initials, OPP, SUB, etc.), and description. Events are colour-coded: green for goals, white for points, grey for wides, orange for opposition scores, yellow for yellow cards, teal for substitutions, blue for period markers, and green-teal for restarts.

Selecting & Removing Events

Tap Select (top right of log) to enter selection mode. Tap individual events to select them, then use the red Remove button to delete them. This is useful for correcting mistakes when Undo isn't sufficient.

Note: Removing events from the log does not automatically recalculate scores. You may need to manually adjust scores after removing scoring events.

9. Stats & Match Analysis

Tap Stats in the bottom bar to open the Stats panel.

Stats — score timeline, momentum split, scoring summary, and match notes

Score Timeline

A line chart showing the running total for both teams over the match. Your team is green, the opposition is red. Dots mark goals (green filled), points (white), 2-pointers (orange), and wides (grey). Dashed lines mark substitutions, red cards, and black cards.

Momentum

A bar and percentage split showing which team has been more dominant, calculated from scores, restarts won, and turnovers (when recorded). A note below the bar indicates which signals are included.

Scoring

A summary card showing total score, conversion rate (scores vs attempts including wides), and a visual breakdown bar.

More stats — placed ball conversion, restart results, and individual player breakdown

Placed Balls

Shows placed ball conversion rate (frees, 65s/45s, penalties) with a ring chart.

Restarts

Breaks down restart results (Won / Lost / Unclear) for both your own and opposition restarts.

Turnovers

When turnover events have been recorded, a Turnovers card shows the total won and lost, a split bar showing the won percentage, and a per-player breakdown.

Shot Map

Visible only when Shot Location Tracking is enabled and at least one shot has been tagged. Shows a full-pitch overhead view with coloured dots for each tagged shot: green for scores (goals shown larger), red for wides. Filter by half (Full Match / H1 / H2) using the chips above the map. A conversion percentage table beneath the map breaks results down by pitch third (Attacking / Middle / Defensive). The Shot Map is also included in the printed/exported match report.

Player Breakdown

Lists individual scoring stats and discipline records (cards).

Match Notes

A text area at the bottom of Stats where you can type free-form notes. These are saved with the match and included in printed reports.

Print / Share

Tap the share icon (top right of Stats panel) to generate a print-friendly match report. This opens the browser's print dialog, producing a professional PDF-style document.

10. Line-up View

From the Setup panel, tap the Line-up button (top right of the Starting players section) to open a visual formation view.

Line-up — formation with shirt numbers, goalkeeper in yellow, captain badge, and subs listed below

This displays your team name, opponent, date, all starting players in their positional formation with numbered shirt icons, the captain with a gold "C" badge, and bench players listed at the bottom. Useful for pre-match team talks or sharing the starting line-up.

12. Undo & Reset

Undo

The Undo button (bottom right, red text) reverses the most recent action — removing the last event and reverting any score, card, or substitution changes. You can undo multiple times, working backward through the stack. Disabled when there are no actions to reverse.

Reset

After a match ends (Full-Time), the Undo button transforms into a Reset button. Tapping it and confirming clears all scores, events, and cards — but preserves team names, player names, and settings, so you can quickly set up the next match.

13. Templates

Templates save your entire squad setup (team name, player names, and captain) for quick reloading.

Saving a Template

  1. Open Setup and ensure all player names are entered.
  2. Type a name in the Template name field (e.g. "Senior Panel 2026").
  3. Tap Save squad.

Loading a Template

Saved templates appear in the Templates section of Setup. Tap Load to populate all player fields. Tap Delete to remove a template. Templates are stored in browser local storage and persist across sessions.

14. Tips & Notes

Screen Wake Lock — While the timer is running, the app requests a screen wake lock to prevent your phone's display from turning off. Released when paused or match ends.

Background Timing — If you switch apps or lock your phone while the timer is running, the clock continues accurately using wall-clock time. The display syncs instantly when you return.

Auto-Save — State is saved every 30 seconds during play, and immediately on pause, page leave, or any action. Reopening the app restores your match with a "Match restored" notification.

Player Initials — The app generates smart initials for the pitch grid. For O' surnames (common in Irish names), it includes enough surname letters to disambiguate. Clashing initials get additional letters automatically.

Hurling vs Football Differences:

  • Football includes the 2 Point score option
  • "From 65" becomes "From 45" in Football
  • "Chop" is not listed as a foul type in Football
  • Restart labels change from "Puck Out" to "Kick Out" in stats

Browser Compatibility — Works in Safari (iOS), Chrome (Android/desktop), Firefox, and Edge. For the best mobile experience, add the page to your home screen to use it like a native app.
